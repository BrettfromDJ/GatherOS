Hide the toolbar on scroll — centered tabs, edge-to-edge grid, floating drop-dock

1 · centered pill cluster
On drag, a compact glass pill of collection chips fades in centered, just under the floating tabs — same centered-pill language as the tabs. Reads as "the tabs grew a tray". No strip, no full width.
state
Best for: consistency — it mirrors the tabs exactly. Watch: the tabs + pill stack vertically at top-center, so two centered things compete for the same zone.
2 · tabs morph into collections
The one centered pill does double duty: drag starts and the Library/Folders/Boards tabs slide out while collection drop-chips slide in, in place. Drop, and it morphs back. Zero new chrome — the same object transforms.
state
Best for: the most elegant — one floating object, two modes. Watch: you briefly lose the tab labels mid-drag (fine, since you're not switching tabs while dragging).
3 · compact centered drop-card
A small frosted card drops down centered, listing collections vertically (mini cover + name + count) like a little menu. Scales to many collections by scrolling inside the card.
state
Best for: libraries with many collections — vertical list + names are easy to aim at. Watch: covers more of the grid's top than a pill.
4 · cursor-anchored cluster
The collection list appears right next to the card you picked up and trails the cursor, so targets are always a short flick away — no traveling to the top of the screen.
state
Best for: least mouse travel — the targets come to you. Watch: it floats over grid content near the cursor and needs smart edge-flipping so it never clips off-screen.
5 · edge-tab reveal
Collections tuck into a slim handle on the right edge while scrolled; drag toward it (or hover) and a compact floating list springs out. Keeps the top totally clean for the tabs + grid.
state
Best for: keeping the top untouched. Watch: least discoverable, and a right-edge throw is a longer drag than a centered target.
